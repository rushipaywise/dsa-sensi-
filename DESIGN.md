# DSA Dojo: Japanese Learning Principles Design

This document outlines the Japanese learning principles integrated into the DSA Dojo to help students beat the "forgetting curve" and achieve mastery.

## 1. Active Recall (Musha Shugyo - 武者修行)
**Concept:** Pulling information out of the brain is more powerful than putting it in.
**Implementation:**
- **Samurai Mode:** In this mode, Sensei will not provide direct answers or code snippets initially. Instead, Sensei will ask Socratic questions to force the student to recall the logic themselves.
- **Challenge Prompts:** "Close your notes. Can you explain the logic of this algorithm in your own words?"

## 2. Kumon Method (Small Steps - 公文式)
**Concept:** Mastery through small, incremental steps (1% improvement daily).
**Implementation:**
- **Learning Paths:** Problems are categorized and ordered by difficulty.
- **Incremental Hints:** Sensei breaks down complex problems into smaller, manageable sub-problems.
- **Progress Tracking:** Visual indicators of small wins and daily streaks.

## 3. Spaced Repetition (Interval Training)
**Concept:** Reviewing information at increasing intervals (5 mins, 3 days, 1 week, 1 month).
**Implementation:**
- **Review Reminders:** The app will highlight problems that are due for review based on the student's last successful attempt.
- **Flashcard Integration:** Key concepts and patterns are presented for quick review.

## 4. Interleaved Practice (Subject Switching)
**Concept:** Switching between different subjects or patterns to improve flexibility.
**Implementation:**
- **Pattern Mixing:** Encouraging students to switch between different DSA patterns (e.g., Arrays to Trees) in a single session.
- **Random Challenge:** A "Samurai Duel" feature that picks a random problem from any mastered category.

## 5. Protege Effect (Sensei Tradition - 先生)
**Concept:** Teaching others makes your own understanding twice as strong.
**Implementation:**
- **Explain to Sensei:** A feature where the student must explain their code to Sensei. Sensei will then provide feedback on the explanation.
- **Peer Review (Simulated):** Sensei might ask, "How would you explain this to a beginner?"

---

## Samurai Mode vs. Normal Mode

| Feature | Normal Mode | Samurai Mode |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Sensei Tone** | Helpful, encouraging mentor. | Strict, challenging master. |
| **Hints** | Direct hints and partial code. | Socratic questions, no code. |
| **Visuals** | Clean, modern UI. | Traditional Japanese aesthetic (Ink wash, Kanji). |
| **Feedback** | Focus on correctness. | Focus on logic, efficiency, and recall. |
